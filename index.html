Mundanities

I read pedantry and pedantry at the college most likely to prima facie (if somewhat unjustly) be seen as the closest analogue to Cowling’s Peterhouse in Oxford.

I welcome correspondence—

  • by post during Full Term: the name and college address will do;
  • by electronic mail: append to my first and surname the college, followed by ‘ox.ac.uk’; and
  • by electronic mail, avoiding the prying eyes of the university: prepend ‘j@’ to this address.

Where did everything go?

There used to be a blog (or compilation of letters) at this address. Its pun of a name was fairly bad when first contrived, and is now increasingly outdated. I shall place the letters somewhere else, when time permits.

Walks

I walk approximately termly (with a slightly worried eye on Hilary) from Oxford to the other major city under the ægis of the most august Thames Valley Police.

Anyone desirous of joining me is welcome to at least give such an indication; the solitude is on occasion welcome, so I reserve the right to refuse such an application.
